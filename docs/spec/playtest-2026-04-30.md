# Paper Playtest — 2026-04-30

A turn-by-turn dry run of the canonical run on Normal-3, played by a hypothetical "average attentive player" with arcade-rail-shooter familiarity but no DORD-specific muscle memory. Friction logged as it would emerge live, with recommended canon edits at the bottom.

This playtest happens **before engine implementation** so issues surface in the design rather than the code.

## The hypothetical player

| Trait | Value |
|---|---|
| Reflexes | 250ms median reaction time |
| Aim | ~75% accuracy on stationary targets at ~10m, ~55% on moving targets |
| Genre fluency | Has played Time Crisis 2 and House of the Dead III in arcades |
| DORD experience | First run |
| Difficulty | Normal-3 (3 lives) |
| Platform | iPhone 14 (touch input) |

## Run trace

### Lobby (target 75s, played 79s)

**Glide-in (t = 0..3s).** The "DEPARTMENT OF REDUNDANCY DEPARTMENT — FLOOR DIRECTORY 1-47" stencilled signage reads quickly. Ambience swell on `radio-chatter` registers as "office-y." Rail tilts forward into Position 1. The player's first instinct is to drag-aim — and the "DRAG TO AIM" narrator overlay arrives 200ms after they start. ✅

**Position 1 — Front Desk (dwell 18s, played 16s).** Door creaks. Manager bursts out. The player headshots him at t=2.5s. Cover-pop manager from side cubicle at t=8s — player covers, peeks, shoots. Background-shamble manager from far depth at t=14s — long-range headshot for skill bonus. Position cleared at t=15s; rail resumes 3s early via `on-clear`. ✅

**Glide to Position 2 (4s).** Player observes wide concourse, two side corridors, a fern, leather couches. Janice painting visible on right wall — player notes it but doesn't fixate.

**Position 2 — Concourse (dwell 22s, played 22s).** Door-burst from north wing manager — kill. Cover-pop office security guard from south wing — kill. Then **the intern walks in N→S at t=10s with no telegraph.** ❗ The player almost shoots; they catch themselves at the last moment. Adrenaline-spike moment. Vault-drop manager from behind couch at t=14s — mid-air headshot lands. Justice-glint cover-pop on left at t=18s — player shoots the weapon-hand, scores 200. ✅ Position cleared.

**Position 3 — Garrison (dwell 22s, played 26s).** Elevator dings. Garrison enters. Clipboard drops with audible *thwack*. Title card: "SECURITY CHIEF GARRISON." Player engages. Phase 1 — Garrison cover-pops from elevator entrance. Player times peeks. Phase 2 at HP 50% — Garrison advances; one ad spawns from side door (Hard+, but Normal might not see this). Player kills Garrison at t=24s. ✅

**Total Lobby: 79s.** 4s over target — Garrison fight ran long because the player wasn't sure when to push offence vs cover. Acceptable.

**Friction:** None severe. The intern walk-through is the only place where civilian recognition is non-trivial; the playtester reports it as "intentional adrenaline" rather than "unfair."

### Stairway A (target 60s, played 64s)

**Tilt transition (4s).** Camera tilts up 25° smoothly. Echoey footsteps on metal start. Ambience drops to ~30% — registers as "service corridor." The player notices the FIRE EXIT sign behind them as they enter the climb.

**Position 1 — Mid-Stair (dwell 22s, played 24s).** Upper landing door bursts at t=2s — manager headshot. Then **the crawler beat fires at t=8s — manager climbs up the stairs from below.** Tutorial overlay: "ENEMIES CAN COME FROM BELOW." The player is mildly surprised but adapts quickly; body shots drop the crawler before lunge. At t=14s, cover-pop manager from same upper door — kill. ✅ But the playtester reports **the wind-up on the crawler felt longer than 1.5s subjectively**, possibly because they were still scanning the upper door for the next beat.

**Climb to Position 2 (12s).** Camera continues climbing. Faint chalk arrow visible on right wall: "AUDITOR ↑". Player notes it.

**Position 2 — Landing Wave (dwell 18s, played 18s).** Both top doors open simultaneously. 4 enemies pour out: 2 managers + 2 security-guards (one is the FIRST OFFICE SECURITY GUARD of the run). Player sweep-fires across both. Title card on the office security guard: "OFFICE SECURITY GUARD" registers but is not lingered on. ✅ Wave clears at t=11s; cover-pop secondary at t=14s of dwell wraps it up.

**Total Stairway A: 64s.** 4s over. Acceptable.

**Friction:** The crawler tutorial overlay arrives **simultaneously** with the crawler emerging — slight read-and-act overlap. Recommend pre-firing the overlay 200ms before the crawler is visible, so the player has it digested.

### Open Plan (target 75s, played 84s)

**Glide-in (3s).** Tilt-down. Ambience swap from `managers-only` to `radio-chatter` — distinct mood shift. The cubicle sea opens up. Player notes the corner office in the distance: "WHITCOMB" glass-walled. Foreshadowing registers immediately.

**Position 1 — Opening Row (dwell 18s, played 19s).** Door-burst office security guard left, background-shamble manager from far depth, vault-drop manager from cubicle row behind, cover-pop office security guard from divider. Four beats in 18s — player feels the tempo step up from Lobby. Played slightly slow on the vault-drop (mid-air shot missed; got it on body shots). Cleared at t=18s of dwell.

**Position 2 — Printer Alley (dwell 22s, played 28s).** ❗ This position runs long.
- **t=2s — Consultant walks N→S.** Player holds fire. ✅
- **t=6s — Printer dolly enters with 2 office security guards on board.** Player tries to sweep-fire but the dolly is moving — **the dismount is hard to read.** Both office security guards survive the entry; one drops them, the other clips the player for 12 HP.
- **t=12s — Door-burst manager from break room.** Easy kill.
- **t=15s — Cover-pop justice-glint manager.** Player makes the disarm shot. ✅
- The position runs over by ~6s because the dolly engagement was messy.

**Position 3 — Whitcomb (dwell 22s, played 28s).** Coffee mug shatters. Title card. Whitcomb fires three-round bursts from desk cover. Phase 1 takes ~14s. Phase 2 transition: Whitcomb leaps onto the desk. Aggressive five-round bursts + 1 ad spawn from cubicle. Player drops one ad, kills Whitcomb at t=26s. ❗ **Player took 35 HP this fight, ending Open Plan at 60% HP.** Higher than ideal — the budget wanted ~75-80% at this point.

**Total Open Plan: 84s.** 9s over.

**Friction:**
1. The vehicle-entry beat at Position 2 was the worst-read beat of the run so far. The dolly is cool but the dismount window (`vehicle-dismount-burst` fire program) reads as "two enemies firing simultaneously" rather than "two enemies dismounting" — visually they're already at fire pose by the time the player aims.
2. Whitcomb's Phase 2 ad spawn is a **player-trapping beat**: while the player is focused on Whitcomb, the ad cover-pops from a side cubicle and lands a free shot. This may be too punishing for first-time Normal players given the cumulative HP loss across Position 2 and Position 3.

### Stairway B (target 90s, played 96s)

**Glide-in.** Ambience drops to ~25%. Concrete walls + chalk graffiti read clearly.

**Position 1 — Propped Door (dwell 28s, played 25s).** The propped-door visual reads — player recognizes "this door was already open." Two enemies emerge from the propped door (`pistol-cover-pop` + `pistol-pop-aim`). Position resolves quickly because the player is already familiar with cover-pop tempo.

**Position 2 — Mass-Pop + Hitman (dwell 32s, played 34s).** All four mass-pop enemies emerge synchronously from left + right top doors. Player sweep-fires. Drops 3 of 4 in window; the 4th (the security-guard from right door) gets a shot off, clipping for 10 HP. **Then at t=12s of dwell:** vending-shutter rolls up, hitman reveal stinger plays, hitman emerges with `pistol-cover-pop`. ❗ **The player almost misses the hitman reveal** because they're still cleanup-firing on the mass-pop. The shutter-up is barely audible over the dying-mass-pop ambience.

Vault-drop swat at t=18s lands on the player's left periphery — they pivot fast, body shots stop the swat. Cleanup hitman with justice-glint at t=24s — player makes the disarm.

**Total Stairway B: 96s.** 6s over.

**Friction:** The hitman-reveal beat needs a stronger audio prelude. Right now `stingers/hitman-reveal.ogg` plays on the same wall-clock tick as the shutter rolls up; the shutter sound and the stinger blur. Recommend the stinger fires 600ms BEFORE the shutter starts moving — gives the player a "something new is coming" cue while they're still mass-popping.

### HR Corridor (target 75s, played 81s)

**Glide-in.** ❗ **Boots go silent.** This is supposed to be sterile-eerie; the playtester reports it as "did the audio break?" — they almost paused. The intentional silence may be too aggressive on first encounter. Recommend a low-volume HVAC hum stays in.

**Position 1 — Silhouette Read (dwell 18s, played 19s).** Three frosted doors open in turn (W1, W2, E2). Player learns the silhouette read — aggressive standing pose = enemy, seated = civilian. The aggressive E2 hitman gets a shot off while the player was processing E2 vs. the passive E1 silhouette. ✅ but tense.

**Position 2 — Hostage Scene (dwell 22s, played 23s).** Three hostages seated, hands raised. Hostile hitman emerges through E3-hostage door, threatens with `hostage-threat` fire program. Player headshots the hitman cleanly — hostages survive. ✅ The playtester explicitly comments "this was the most stressful 15 seconds of the run."

**Position 3 — Phelps (dwell 22s, played 26s).** Power-out cue fires; lights snap to 0. Phelps spotlight snaps on. "I have your *file*, auditor" plays. Player engages. Phase 1 — Phelps shoots from behind cabinet cover; player breaks cabinet via 5 hits, exposes Phelps. Phase 2 — binder lobs (`lob-throw`); player shoots one mid-arc, takes one impact for 25 HP. ❗ **The lob arc on Normal feels TOO slow** — the player reports they had time for "two shots, easy" between lobs. Recommend tightening the loop interval from 2.5s to 2.0s.

**Total HR Corridor: 81s.** 6s over.

**Friction:**
1. Boots-go-silent is too aggressive on first play — adopt a low-volume HVAC layer.
2. Phelps Phase 2 lob frequency too generous on Normal.

### Stairway C (target 120s, played 132s)

**Glide-in.** Camera tilts +30° (steeper than A and B), ambience swaps to slow grandfather-clock tick. ❗ **The camera roll oscillation (±2°)** is reported as "unsettling" — playtester says they thought their phone was at a weird angle. Effect lands as designed. ✅

**Position 1 — Painting Hidden-Door (dwell 22s, played 24s).** Painting creak audio cue fires. Painting hinges out, revealing a hidden door with a hitman behind it. Player engages. Then the mid-1-L door opens, security-guard cover-pops. Two enemies cleared at t=22s of dwell.

**Position 2 — Sniper + Crawler (dwell 28s, played 32s).** ❗ **Multi-axis priority is genuinely hard.** Sniper rises from upper bannister; crawler emerges from lower well; two security-guards burst from mid-2-L door. Player tries to prioritize sniper (35 damage punisher) but takes a body shot from one security-guard. Then almost gets crawled. Body-shots the crawler late, 20 HP loss. Position cleared at t=28s of dwell — but the player is now at **42% HP heading into Position 3.**

**Position 3 — Ad-Rush + Chandelier (dwell 35s, played 38s).** Power-out cue fires (mood-locks). Three doors open in sequence (mid-2-R, mid-2-S, mid-2-R again with hitman). Player burns the chandelier at t=8s (via 5 quick chandelier hits). The chandelier crash takes out 2 of the 3 ads in radius; player cleans up the third manually. ✅ Chandelier-as-tactical-object lands. **Player ends Stairway C at 35% HP — pre-boss attrition target was 60-80%.** ❗ **This level is over-tuned at Normal.**

**Total Stairway C: 132s.** 12s over.

**Friction:**
1. Position 2 dual-axis priority is borderline UN-difficulty fair on Normal. Recommend either: extend sniper wind-up from 1.5s to 2.0s on Normal, OR reduce crawler frequency to 1 (currently 1, which is the floor — so go with sniper extension).
2. Position 3 ad-rush of 3 enemies + sniper-cleanup-from-Position-2 is too dense. Recommend dropping the third ad-rush spawn (mid-2-R repeat) on Normal, keep on Hard+.

### Executive Suites (target 75s, played 80s)

**Glide-in.** Panic-alarm wail starts. Mahogany walls, red-carpet. Ambience swap to `tense-drone` at 0.65 volume — palpable.

**Position 1 — Reception (dwell 18s, played 18s).** Two doors open + vault. Three enemies dispatched cleanly. ✅

**Position 2 — Lounge (dwell 22s, played 28s).** Pre-aggro hitman visible BEFORE alert — player notices, scans, fires first shot. Hitman `idle` program transitions to fire on player's first shot (taps a "alerted" condition). ❗ **The pre-aggro tape transition** is noted: in the current cue list, it's `enemy-spawn` with `idle` and a planned 2.5s flip to `pistol-pop-aim`. **Implementation note: the fire program needs an `on-player-fire` event, which is not in the canonical fire-program event list (`02-encounter-vocabulary.md`).** This is a gap in the canon — recommend adding `pre-aggro-pistol-pop` as a fire program with built-in 2.5s idle prefix.

Doors open; lounge enemies dispatched. Vent-drop swat at t=14s (cue at wall-clock 51s) — heavy but fair. Position cleared at t=22s. **Player ends at 30% HP — too low for Crawford fight.**

**Position 3 — Crawford (dwell 22s, played 24s).** Bourbon set down. Spotlight snaps on. Shotgun rack. Crawford at desk (Phase 1). Phase 1 — Crawford shotgun blasts; player times peeks. Phase 2 — Crawford flips desk, advances. Vent-drop ad. Crawford dies at t=23s. ✅ But player ended the level at **22% HP — well into "one shot from death" territory** going into the Boardroom.

**Total Executive Suites: 80s.** 5s over. **Cumulative attrition is the issue.**

**Friction:**
1. The `pre-aggro` fire program is not in the canon. Adopt `pre-aggro-pistol-pop` as a new preset with `idle 2500ms` prefix before `pistol-pop-aim`.
2. Cumulative attrition from Stairway C → Executive Suites is too steep. Either raise base player HP by 15-20%, or add a brief "pre-Boardroom safe room" beat at the end of Executive Suites where the player gets ~30 HP back via a held wall-clock dwell (a brief silence — would be tonal, not gameplay-mechanical).

### Boardroom (target 60s, played 95s)

**Approach (4s).** 20-foot mahogany doors creak open. Ambience drops to 0. Reaper silhouette visible at far end. Doors slam shut behind player at t=4s — thunderclap, dust-puff, camera-shake. Player jumps. ✅ **The reveal lands.**

**Phase 1 — REDACT (~24s).** Reaper at far end. Boss theme drops. Redaction bars sweep across screen, masking 30% of the play area. Player iPad-shoots at t=8s, breaks redact mechanic for the rest of Phase 1. Two ads from ceiling vents at t=12s. ✅ Phase 1 cleared at t=24s. **Player at 8% HP.**

**Phase 2 — TELEPORT (~28s).** Reaper teleports between A/B/C/D corners. Player learns the afterimage tell. Scythe-slash on close. ❗ **Player dies on first scythe-slash at Phase 2 t=14s.** Continue prompt fires.

**Continue 1 — restart from Phase 2 (~30s).** Player retries Phase 2. This time they kite teleports better, drop Reaper to 50%. Phase 3 entry triggers.

**Phase 3 — SUBPOENA (~22s).** Subpoena lobs (`lob-throw` arcing projectiles), mass-pop ads from ceiling + floor. Player burns scythe (justice-shot during Phase 3) at t=8s, halving ad rate. Reaper dies at t=22s. **Hood-falls-back. Empty robe collapses. Scythe clatters. Run cleared.**

**Total Boardroom: 95s** (60s authored + 35s continue penalty).

**Friction:**
1. Phase 2 scythe-slash on Normal is the run's "git gud" beat. First-attempt clear rate target was ~50% — playtester took two attempts, which is consistent with target. Acceptable.
2. The continue prompt → restart-from-Phase-2 is correct behaviour. Don't restart from Phase 1.

## Friction summary & recommended canon edits

### High priority (canon edit before engine implementation)

1. **`pre-aggro-pistol-pop` fire program is missing from `02-encounter-vocabulary.md`.** The Executive Suites Position 2 design requires it. Add it: `idle 2500ms → pistol-pop-aim` non-loop. The `idle` event needs to break on a director-emitted "alert" signal (player fires, or 5s timeout). This implies the fire-program event spec needs an `on-alert` extension.

2. **Vehicle-entry dismount is hard to read.** Recommend adding a 200ms "flinch" pose at the end of the vehicle's traverse rail before enemies start firing, so the player has a visual beat between "vehicle stops" and "enemies fire." Edit needed: `02-encounter-vocabulary.md` Vehicle-entry beat description, and the `vehicle-dismount-burst` fire program — add `idle 200ms` prefix.

3. **Hitman-reveal stinger needs to lead the shutter motion by ~600ms.** Edit needed: Stairway B cue list — split the `p2-shutter` and `p2-hitman-A` cues so the stinger fires 600ms before the shutter rolls up.

### Medium priority

4. **Crawler tutorial overlay should pre-fire 200ms before the crawler is visible.** Edit Stairway A cue list: add `wall-clock` cue at +6800ms (relative to position dwell start) for `narrator: 'ENEMIES CAN COME FROM BELOW'`, before the existing `enemy-spawn` cue at +7000ms.

5. **HR Corridor "boots go silent" needs a low-volume HVAC layer.** Edit HR Corridor cue list: add `ambience-fade` to a new `hvac-hum` layer at intensity 0.15 (rather than dropping all ambience to silent).

6. **Phelps Phase 2 binder-lob loop interval too generous on Normal.** Edit `02-encounter-vocabulary.md` `lob-throw` fire program: tighten loop interval from 2.5s to 2.0s.

7. **Stairway C Position 2 sniper wind-up should extend on Normal.** Edit Stairway C cue list / `sniper-aim` fire program: per-difficulty wind-up override for Normal goes from 1.5s to 1.8s. (Hard+ stays at 1.5s.)

8. **Stairway C Position 3 third ad-rush spawn is too dense on Normal.** Edit Stairway C cue list: gate `p3-rush-3` cue on Hard+. Implementation: add `difficulty: 'hard+'` field to the cue (extension to `05-screenplay-language.md` cue schema). OR: the director honours difficulty filtering at level-construct.

### Low priority (post-engine playtest validation)

9. **Cumulative attrition Stairway C → Boardroom is too steep on Normal.** Either raise player HP by 15-20%, or add a "safe room" tonal-pause at the end of Executive Suites. Decision deferred to live-playtest data — paper-playtest is consistent with target attrition curve, so this may resolve naturally.

10. **Whitcomb Phase 2 ad spawn is a player-trapping beat.** Consider widening the cubicle ad-spawn rail's wind-up time on Normal (from `pistol-cover-pop`'s 0.5s wind-up to 0.8s on this specific instance). Or accept as designed — Whitcomb is the second mini-boss, increased pressure is intentional.

## Schema extensions required

Two extensions to the canonical schema surfaced in playtest:

1. **`pre-aggro-pistol-pop` fire program** (`02-encounter-vocabulary.md`): add to the fire-program table.
2. **Per-cue difficulty filter** (`05-screenplay-language.md`): add optional `difficulty?: 'easy+' | 'normal+' | 'hard+' | 'nightmare+' | 'un-only'` field to the `Cue` interface.

These edits should land before the engine implements the cue machinery.

## Verdict

The run shape is **structurally sound**. Pacing arc is felt — Lobby gentle → tip-over at HR → Reaper climax. The construction primitives + screenplay model + dumb-prop architecture all hang together; nothing in this paper-playtest required ad-hoc machinery outside the locked vocabulary.

The friction list is normal first-paper-playtest scope: 8 specific edits, 2 schema extensions, 0 architectural rewrites. Apply the edits, then proceed to engine implementation.
